üü© Early Years (1984 ‚Äì 1990s)

The first edition of the Rothmans Asia Cup was held in 1984 in Sharjah, UAE, the location of the headquarters of the newly formed Asian Cricket Council. The tournament was a round-robin contest among India, Sri Lanka, and Pakistan. The first match was between Pakistan and the new ICC member Sri Lanka. India won the tournament with two victories, Sri Lanka finished runners-up with a single win over Pakistan, while Pakistan failed to win either of its two games.

Sri Lanka hosted the second edition in 1986. India withdrew from the tournament due to strained cricketing relations with Sri Lanka after a controversial series the previous year. Bangladesh participated for the first time. Sri Lanka won the tournament by defeating Pakistan in the final. The third edition, in 1988, was held in Bangladesh, marking the first time a multi-national cricket tournament was staged there. In the final, India beat Sri Lanka by six wickets to claim their second Asia Cup.

üüß Legacy

The legacy of the Asia Cup stands as a powerful testament to the unity and evolution of Asian cricket. Since its launch in 1984, the tournament has provided a unique continental platform, allowing teams not only to compete for supremacy but also to foster friendships, rivalries, and national pride. By regularly bringing together cricket giants like India, Pakistan, Sri Lanka, and emerging nations such as Bangladesh and Afghanistan, the Asia Cup has accelerated the growth of the sport across the region. It has served as an arena for legendary battles, stunning upsets, and the rise of new superstars, often acting as a stepping stone for teams and players toward greater global recognition.
